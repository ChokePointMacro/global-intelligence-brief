## 1. Critical Minerals & Rare Earth Elements

### Rare Earth Elements (REE)
- China mines **~60% of global rare earths** and processes **~85–90%** of global supply
- Key elements: neodymium, praseodymium (EV motors, wind turbines), dysprosium (high-temp magnets), terbium (LEDs, lasers), lanthanum (catalysts, optics)
- China imposed rare earth export quotas in 2010–2012 (Japan dispute), causing prices to spike 2,000%+ before WTO ruling
- **2023:** China restricted exports of gallium and germanium (semiconductor precursors), directly targeting US/EU chipmakers
- **2024:** China banned exports of antimony (flame retardants, military munitions), added to gallium/germanium restrictions
- **2025:** China expanded controls on tungsten, indium, bismuth, molybdenum — all critical for defense and semiconductor manufacturing
- Cascade: REE restriction → no neodymium magnets → no EV motors, no wind turbine generators, no F-35 actuators, no MRI machines

### Gallium & Germanium
- China produces **~80% of global gallium**, **~60% of germanium**
- Used in: compound semiconductors (GaAs, GaN), infrared optics, fiber optic cables, solar cells, military radar
- Export license requirement since August 2023; US, Japan, Germany are most exposed
- No near-term Western alternative; US has one gallium producer (small scale)
- Cascade: gallium cutoff → compound semiconductor fabs halt → 5G base station shortage → defense radar gap

### Cobalt
- China controls **~70% of cobalt refining** (Democratic Republic of Congo mines ~70% of raw ore; China owns most DRC mines)
- Used in: lithium-ion battery cathodes (NMC chemistry), aerospace superalloys, cutting tools
- Price spike 2017–2018 (+300%) caused EV battery cost inflation globally
- Companies exposed: CATL, BYD (vertically integrated); Apple, Tesla, GM (dependent on spot market)

### Lithium
- China controls **~60% of lithium processing/refining** despite not being top lithium miner
- Australia, Chile, Argentina mine lithium; China converts it to battery-grade lithium hydroxide/carbonate
- Sociedad Química y Minera (SQM) of Chile sold 22% stake to Tianqi Lithium (Chinese) in 2018
- CATL controls largest lithium mining operations in Bolivia, Zimbabwe, Argentina

### Graphite
- China produces **~65% of natural graphite** and **~90%+ of processed anode graphite** (battery grade)
- Every lithium-ion battery anode is ~99% graphite; no commercial substitute at scale
- **October 2023:** China imposed export controls on all graphite products
- US has zero domestic battery-grade graphite processing capacity as of 2025
- Cascade: graphite restriction → battery anode shortage → EV production halts in 6–9 months → GM, Ford, Tesla, Rivian production collapse

## 2. Agricultural Inputs & Food Security

### Phosphate Fertilizer
- China produces **~35–40% of global phosphate fertilizer** (DAP, MAP, TSP)
- China banned phosphate exports in **October 2021** to control domestic food prices — global DAP prices rose 80% in 3 months
- Export ban was lifted in 2022 but restrictions remain via quota system
- Morocco (OCP Group) controls ~70% of world's phosphate rock reserves — China has invested heavily
- Cascade: phosphate shortage → reduced crop yields → food inflation 6–18 months later → political instability in food-import-dependent nations (Egypt, Pakistan, Sub-Saharan Africa)

### Potash
- China is top potash consumer; major supplier relationship with Russia (Uralkali) and Belarus (Belaruskali)
- Western sanctions on Belarus in 2021 removed ~15% of global potash supply — prices tripled
- China locked in long-term contracts below market price; Western buyers paid spot premiums
- China holds strategic potash reserves and has domestic Qinghai/Xinjiang deposits

### Nitrogen Fertilizer (Urea)
- China is world's largest urea producer; export policy directly controls global pricing
- **2021:** China restricted urea exports ahead of domestic planting season; global prices +150%
- India, Bangladesh, Vietnam, and Sub-Saharan Africa most exposed — cannot afford spot price spikes

### Food Cascade Model
1. China restricts fertilizer exports (phosphate + urea simultaneously)
2. Developing world farmers reduce application rates → yields fall 15–30%
3. 6-month lag → global grain stocks tighten (wheat, corn, rice)
4. Food price index rises 40–60% → import-dependent nations face social unrest
5. Historical precedent: 2007–2008 food crisis (not China-caused but same cascade mechanism) → riots in 30+ countries

## 3. Pharmaceutical & Healthcare Supply Chain

### Active Pharmaceutical Ingredients (APIs)
- China supplies **~60–80% of global APIs** (estimates vary; US FDA data suggests 80%+ for common generics)
- India manufactures ~40% of global generic drugs but sources 70%+ of its APIs from China
- Key drugs dependent on Chinese APIs: amoxicillin, azithromycin, ibuprofen, aspirin, metformin (diabetes), heparin (blood thinner), most antibiotics
- **2020 COVID shock:** India temporarily banned API exports → global generic drug shortage alert
- US has no domestic penicillin fermentation capacity as of 2025

### Critical Drug Dependencies
- **Heparin:** ~80% of raw material (porcine intestine mucosa) comes from Chinese pigs; 2008 contamination scandal killed 81 Americans
- **Vitamin C:** China controls ~90% of global ascorbic acid production (used in food, pharma, supplements)
- **Paracetamol/Acetaminophen precursor (PARA):** China dominates; shortage in 2022 caused European over-the-counter drug shortages
- Cascade: China API restriction → Indian generic manufacturers halt → Western hospital formularies disrupted in 3–6 months → elective surgeries cancelled → ICU drug shortages in 12 months

## 4. Semiconductor & Electronics Supply Chain

### Advanced Packaging & Substrates
- China controls significant portions of PCB (printed circuit board) manufacturing — **~50% of global PCB output**
- Taiwan (TSMC) fabricates leading-edge chips but depends on Chinese substrate suppliers for advanced packaging
- OSAT (outsourced semiconductor assembly and test): China hosts ~25% of global capacity

### Rare Earth Magnets in Electronics
- Neodymium-iron-boron (NdFeB) magnets: China produces **~90% globally**
- Used in: every hard drive, EV motor, wind turbine generator, military guidance systems, loudspeakers, MRI machines
- Japan's TDK and Shin-Etsu Chemical are largest non-Chinese producers — still source Chinese rare earths

### Legacy Chip Manufacturing
- China is largest single market for semiconductors (~35% of global consumption)
- SMIC (China's leading fab) at 7nm node; cutting-edge but supply-restricted by US export controls
- Huawei's Kirin 9000S (made by SMIC at 7nm) demonstrated China can partially circumvent controls

### ZPMC Port Cranes — Intelligence Concern
- Shanghai Zhenhua Heavy Industries (ZPMC) manufactures **~70% of ship-to-shore cranes worldwide**
- US ports: Port of Baltimore, Los Angeles, Seattle operate ZPMC cranes
- **2023:** US Congress investigated ZPMC cranes for embedded cellular modems with remote access capability
- **2024:** Biden administration announced replacement program; USCG issued security directive on Chinese-made port equipment

## 5. Electric Vehicle & Battery Supply Chain

### Battery Cell Manufacturing
- China controls **~75% of global lithium-ion battery cell manufacturing** (CATL ~37% alone, BYD ~16%)
- CATL supplies BMW, Volkswagen, Tesla (Model 3/Y), Ford (F-150 Lightning via BlueOval SK JV), Stellantis
- LFP (lithium iron phosphate) chemistry is Chinese-dominated; Tesla switched to LFP for standard range in 2021

### Battery Materials Processing
- Cathode active material (CAM): China processes ~70%
- Anode: ~90% (graphite, as above)
- Electrolyte: China produces ~80% of lithium hexafluorophosphate (LiPF6) salt
- Separator: China produces ~60% globally
- Full dependency chain: if China restricts battery materials → Western EV factories run out of cells in 3–6 months → EV transition stalls globally

### IRA & Friend-Shoring
- US Inflation Reduction Act (2022) requires battery materials from "free trade agreement" countries by 2027 to qualify for $7,500 EV tax credit
- No FTA with critical mineral suppliers (DRC, Indonesia, Zimbabwe) yet
- CATL attempting US factory via licensing/JV structures to qualify for IRA incentives

## 6. Chinese Infrastructure Investments (Strategic Leverage Points)

### Port Infrastructure (Belt & Road)
- China owns or has significant stakes in **90+ ports across 50+ countries**
- Key strategic ports: Hambantota (Sri Lanka, 99-year lease), Djibouti (Horn of Africa), Gwadar (Pakistan, CPEC), Piraeus (Greece, COSCO), Darwin (Australia, 99-year lease to Landbridge)
- Concern: in conflict scenario, China could deny port access to Western shipping or provide intelligence on vessel movements

### COSCO Shipping
- COSCO is world's 3rd largest container shipping company
- Owns terminals in Rotterdam, Antwerp, Hamburg (49%), Barcelona, Valencia, Piraeus
- EU debated restricting COSCO terminal ownership in 2023 after port security concerns
- Could theoretically cause major congestion at European ports by slowing throughput

### Telecommunications & Undersea Cables
- Huawei Marine (now HMN Technologies) built ~25% of world's undersea cable network
- China has significant stake in cables connecting Southeast Asia, Middle East, Africa, Europe
- Huawei/ZTE 5G network presence in 170+ countries despite US lobbying
- "Clean Network" initiative (US) pushed back on Huawei but many developing nations still deploying

## 7. Hidden Single-Point-of-Failure Dependencies (Underreported)

### Ascorbic Acid (Vitamin C)
- China controls ~90% of global production; market cartelized 2001–2008 (US DOJ conviction)
- Used not just in supplements but as food preservative in nearly all processed food globally

### Citric Acid
- China produces ~70% of global citric acid (used in every soda, candy, processed food, cleaning products, pharmaceuticals)

### Magnesium Metal
- China produces **~85% of global magnesium**
- Used in: aluminum alloys (car bodies, aircraft fuselage), die casting for auto parts
- **2021:** Yunnan province power rationing cut output 50%; global magnesium price +300%; automotive industry warned of production halts within weeks
- European Aluminium association warned of factory shutdowns if crisis continued

### Solar Panel Supply Chain
- China controls **~80–85% of every stage** of solar panel manufacturing (polysilicon, wafers, cells, modules)
- Xinjiang produces ~35% of global polysilicon; US/EU import bans (Uyghur Forced Labor Prevention Act) created near-term supply shock
- US solar installations fell in 2022 due to customs detentions under UFLPA

### Acetonitrile
- China dominates acetonitrile production (byproduct of acrylonitrile manufacturing)
- Used as solvent in pharmaceutical production; shortage in 2009 (financial crisis reduced acrylonitrile output) halted drug manufacturing globally
